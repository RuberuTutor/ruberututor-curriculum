# Grade 8 Language Curriculum Map

---

## Strand A: Literacy Connections and Applications
### Overall Expectation
- Apply transferable skills, digital media literacy, cross-curricular connections, and cultural awareness in literacy contexts.

**A1: Transferable Skills**
- A1.1 analyze and explain how transferable skills (critical thinking, innovation, collaboration, communication, global citizenship, digital literacy) support communication in cultural, social, linguistic, and domain-specific contexts  
- A1.2 evaluate how transferable skills help them express voice, stay engaged, and develop capabilities, and create a plan for growth  

**A2: Digital Media Literacy**
- A2.1 evaluate and explain rights/responsibilities when interacting online, making decisions that foster positive digital identities  
- A2.2 demonstrate safe, ethical, and well-being–focused navigation of digital environments  
- A2.3 conduct research considering accuracy, credibility, bias, misinformation, and disinformation, while respecting legal/ethical standards  
- A2.4 analyze forms, conventions, and techniques in media texts and apply them to their own creations  
- A2.5 evaluate relationships among form, message, audience, and purpose  
- A2.6 use digital tools to solve problems and design creative works  
- A2.7 collaborate in online communities inclusively and responsibly  

**A3: Applications, Connections, and Contributions**
- A3.1 apply language skills across subject areas and real-world contexts  
- A3.2 engage with diverse voices, perspectives, and Canadian contexts  
- A3.3 interpret themes in First Nations, Métis, and Inuit cultures to build deeper understanding of identities, legacies, and ways of knowing

## Strand B: Foundations of Language
### Overall Expectation
- Strengthen oral communication, reading, writing, and conventions for effective communication.

**B1: Oral and Non-Verbal Communication**
- B1.1 evaluate listening skills, including formulating thoughtful questions and responses  
- B1.2 analyze listening strategies before, during, and after listening to comprehend/respond critically  
- B1.3 determine purpose/audience and select speaking strategies accordingly  
- B1.4 use advanced oral and non-verbal strategies (tone, gestures, pacing) for rhetorical effect, with cultural awareness  
- B1.5 use precise and varied language forms to convey meaning in formal and informal contexts  

**B2: Language Foundations for Reading and Writing**
- B2.1 apply advanced morphological strategies (e.g., Greek/Latin roots) to decode and spell complex words  
- B2.2 expand vocabulary flexibly and accurately in diverse contexts  
- B2.3 read increasingly complex texts fluently and expressively, adjusting to purpose and audience  

**B3: Language Conventions**
- B3.1 construct complex, multi-clause sentences with cohesive devices for clarity  
- B3.2 demonstrate understanding of advanced grammar (verbals, appositives, complex modifiers, rhetorical structures)  
- B3.3 apply punctuation/capitalization for sophistication and clarity (colons, semicolons, dashes, ellipses, parallel structures)  

---

## Strand C: Comprehension – Understanding and Responding to Texts
### Overall Expectation
- Apply comprehension strategies to analyze and evaluate meaning in diverse texts.

**C1: Knowledge about Texts**
- C1.1 comprehend complex texts using vocabulary, grammar, cohesion, and schema  
- C1.2 analyze a wide range of text forms/genres, including cultural and digital, for structure and meaning  
- C1.3 evaluate text patterns (argument, comparison) and features (annotations, accessible design) for comprehension  
- C1.4 analyze how visual elements/design contribute to meaning across media  
- C1.5 evaluate stylistic devices (tone, diction, figurative language, rhetorical devices) for effect and purpose  
- C1.6 assess how point of view influences meaning, including shifts in perspective or unreliable narrators  
- C1.7 interpret texts by First Nations, Métis, and Inuit creators, engaging in Indigenous Storywork to connect with lived experiences and cultural knowledge  

**C2: Comprehension Strategies**
- C2.1 apply pre-reading strategies (questioning, previewing, anticipating arguments)  
- C2.2 summarize, synthesize, and critique information after/during reading  
- C2.3 monitor comprehension, self-correct, and apply advanced fix-up strategies  

**C3: Critical Thinking in Literacy**
- C3.1 critically evaluate texts, supporting arguments with evidence  
- C3.2 create nuanced text-to-self, text-to-world, and text-to-text connections  
- C3.3 evaluate inclusion and omission of voices, perspectives, and power structures  
- C3.4 reflect on how exposure to diverse texts shapes identity, perspective, and action  

---

## Strand D: Composition – Expressing Ideas and Creating Texts
### Overall Expectation
- Plan, draft, refine, and present sophisticated texts for varied purposes and audiences.

**D1: Developing Ideas**
- D1.1 generate ideas through advanced inquiry and creative processes  
- D1.2 plan structures using complex organizers and frameworks  
- D1.3 research, evaluate, and organize information for cohesion and impact  
- D1.4 reflect on effectiveness of planning and idea-generation strategies  

**D2: Creating Texts**
- D2.1 draft extended texts (analytical essays, narratives, speeches, multimodal texts) with clear purpose and audience  
- D2.2 use fluent digital and traditional writing skills, including multimedia composition  
- D2.3 establish and sustain a distinctive voice, adjusting tone and register as appropriate  
- D2.4 critically evaluate their own point of view, acknowledging bias and perspective  
- D2.5 revise for depth, style, and clarity; edit for accuracy in grammar, spelling, and usage  

**D3: Publishing, Presenting, and Reflecting**
- D3.1 produce polished, sophisticated texts in multiple formats/media  
- D3.2 present ideas clearly with rhetorical awareness, style, and effectiveness  
- D3.3 critically reflect on process, strategies, and growth as text creators  

---

## Cross-Strand Task (optional)
**Example**: *“Social Justice Inquiry Project”* – students analyze persuasive media texts for bias (C3), research with attention to credibility (A2.3), compose an argumentative essay/speech (D2), apply grammar and conventions (B3), and present findings digitally with multimedia (A2, D3).
