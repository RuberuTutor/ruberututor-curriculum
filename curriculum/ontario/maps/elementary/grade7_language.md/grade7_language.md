# Grade 7 Language Curriculum Map

---

## Strand A: Literacy Connections and Applications
### Overall Expectation
- Apply transferable skills, digital media literacy, cross-curricular connections, and cultural awareness in literacy contexts.

**A1: Transferable Skills**
- A1.1: explain how transferable skills (critical thinking, innovation, collaboration, communication, global citizenship, digital literacy) are used to support communication in cultural, social, linguistic, and subject-specific contexts  
- A1.2: explain how transferable skills help express voice, engage in learning, and plan next steps for growth  

**A2: Digital Media Literacy**
- A2.1: explain rights/responsibilities when developing a digital identity and interacting online  
- A2.2: manage online safety, privacy, and digital well-being  
- A2.3: gather, evaluate, and use information considering credibility, accuracy, and perspectives  
- A2.4: analyze forms/techniques in media texts and apply them to their own creations  
- A2.5: explain relationships between form, message, audience, and purpose  
- A2.6: use digital tools for problem-solving and creative design  
- A2.7: collaborate respectfully and inclusively with online communities  

**A3: Applications, Connections, and Contributions**
- A3.1: apply language skills across subject areas and real-world contexts  
- A3.2: engage with diverse voices, perspectives, and Canadian contexts  
- A3.3: analyze themes in First Nations, Métis, and Inuit cultures to deepen understanding of identities, legacies, and ways of knowing  

---

## Strand B: Foundations of Language
### Overall Expectation
- Strengthen oral communication, reading, writing, and conventions for effective communication.

**B1: Oral and Non-Verbal Communication**
- B1.1: analyze effective listening skills (asking relevant questions, responding appropriately)  
- B1.2: select and use listening strategies before, during, and after listening to comprehend and respond  
- B1.3: identify purpose/audience and choose speaking strategies accordingly  
- B1.4: use oral and non-verbal strategies (tone, gestures, body language) for impact, considering cultural variation  
- B1.5: use precise vocabulary, varied sentence structures, and active/passive voice in formal and informal contexts  

**B2: Language Foundations for Reading and Writing**
- B2.1: apply morphological knowledge (prefixes, suffixes, bases) to decode and spell complex words  
- B2.2: expand vocabulary flexibly across contexts, using morphological strategies for new words  
- B2.3: read complex texts fluently, with accuracy and appropriate pacing, adjusting expression and intonation to purpose  

**B3: Language Conventions**
- B3.1: construct complex sentences with multiple clauses and cohesive ties  
- B3.2: understand and apply functions of parts of speech (indirect objects, predicate nouns/adjectives, participles, adverbial phrases)  
- B3.3: apply capitalization/punctuation to communicate meaning (colons, semicolons, conjunctive adverbs, ellipses, dashes)  

---

## Strand C: Comprehension – Understanding and Responding to Texts
### Overall Expectation
- Apply comprehension strategies to analyze and interpret meaning in diverse texts.

**C1: Knowledge about Texts**
- C1.1: read and comprehend complex texts using grammar, cohesion, and background knowledge  
- C1.2: analyze a variety of forms and genres, including cultural texts, for meaning  
- C1.3: analyze patterns (cause/effect) and features (bibliographies, accessible fonts) in texts  
- C1.4: analyze how images and design features communicate meaning  
- C1.5: analyze stylistic elements (voice, word choice, sentence structure) and their role in creating meaning  
- C1.6: identify point of view (first, second, third person), explain how shifts in perspective change meaning  
- C1.7: engage with texts by First Nations, Métis, and Inuit creators to build understanding through Indigenous Storywork  

**C2: Comprehension Strategies**
- C2.1: apply pre-reading strategies (activating prior knowledge, predicting)  
- C2.2: summarize, paraphrase, and synthesize ideas during/after reading  
- C2.3: monitor comprehension, applying fix-up strategies when necessary  

**C3: Critical Thinking in Literacy**
- C3.1: evaluate texts and provide evidence-based opinions  
- C3.2: make text-to-self, text-to-text, and text-to-world connections  
- C3.3: analyze inclusion and omission of perspectives in texts  
- C3.4: reflect on how diverse voices shape their own thinking  

---

## Strand D: Composition – Expressing Ideas and Creating Texts
### Overall Expectation
- Plan, draft, revise, edit, and present a range of texts effectively.

**D1: Developing Ideas**
- D1.1: generate ideas using inquiry, reflection, and research  
- D1.2: use organizers/strategies to plan text structures  
- D1.3: gather, assess, and organize information logically  
- D1.4: reflect on idea generation and organization strategies  

**D2: Creating Texts**
- D2.1: draft extended narrative, persuasive, and informational texts using various forms/media  
- D2.2: demonstrate fluency in keyboarding, cursive, and digital writing  
- D2.3: establish and maintain voice, adjusting tone/style for purpose and audience  
- D2.4: identify bias and point of view in their writing  
- D2.5: revise texts for coherence, organization, and precision  
- D2.6: edit for grammar, punctuation, and spelling  

**D3: Publishing, Presenting, and Reflecting**
- D3.1: produce polished texts using varied media/techniques  
- D3.2: present texts effectively with clear communication choices  
- D3.3: reflect on tools and strategies that supported publishing/presentation  

---

## Cross-Strand Task (optional)
**Example**: *“Cultural Perspectives Multimedia Project”* – students read Indigenous and global texts (C1.7, A3.2), analyze perspectives (C3), draft scripts/articles (D2), apply conventions (B3), and present using digital platforms (A2, D3).
