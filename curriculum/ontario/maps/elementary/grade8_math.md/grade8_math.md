## Strand B: Number
### B1: Number Sense
- B1.1: read, represent, compose, and decompose whole numbers up to billions  
- B1.2: compare and order whole numbers and decimal numbers up to millionths  
- B1.3: solve problems involving percents greater than 100% and less than 1%  
- B1.4: represent, compare, and order fractions, decimals, and percents, and describe relationships among them  
- B1.5: use scientific notation to express very large and very small numbers  
- B1.6: describe and represent square roots of perfect squares up to 225  

### B2: Operations
- B2.1: use properties of operations to solve problems with whole numbers, fractions, decimals, and integers  
- B2.2: multiply and divide fractions, decimals, and integers  
- B2.3: solve multi-step problems involving operations with whole numbers, fractions, decimals, percents, and integers  
- B2.4: evaluate expressions involving order of operations with whole numbers, fractions, decimals, integers, and exponents  
- B2.5: apply proportional reasoning to solve problems involving rates, ratios, and scale factors:contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

## Strand C: Algebra
- C1.1: represent linear relations using tables, graphs, and equations  
- C1.2: model real-life linear situations using algebraic expressions and equations  
- C2.1: solve equations with whole numbers, fractions, decimals, integers, and exponents  
- C2.2: solve inequalities and graph solutions on a number line  
- C3.1: evaluate and simplify algebraic expressions involving exponents and integers  

---

## Strand D: Data
### D1: Data Literacy
- D1.1: collect, organize, and display data using appropriate sampling techniques  
- D1.2: create and interpret bar graphs, histograms, scatter plots, circle graphs, and line graphs  
- D1.3: calculate and compare mean, median, mode, and range, and describe how they are affected by outliers  
- D1.4: analyze data to draw conclusions and justify decisions  

### D2: Probability
- D2.1: determine theoretical and experimental probabilities of compound events involving two or more independent events  
- D2.2: represent probabilities as fractions, decimals, and percents; plot on a probability line:contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}  

---

## Strand E: Spatial Sense
### E1: Geometric and Spatial Reasoning
- E1.1: classify and construct polygons and 3D figures using properties of sides, angles, diagonals, and symmetry  
- E1.2: determine angle relationships involving parallel lines, transversals, and polygons  
- E1.3: perform and describe transformations (translations, reflections, rotations, dilations) on the Cartesian plane:contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}  

### E2: Measurement
- E2.1: represent very large and very small metric units using scientific notation (e.g., nano-, giga-)  
- E2.2: convert between metric units, including square and cubic units  
- E2.3: solve problems involving perimeter, area, volume, and surface area of prisms, pyramids, cylinders, and spheres  
- E2.4: apply formulas for circumference and area of circles  

---

## Strand F: Financial Literacy
- F1.1: compare interest rates and fees for different financial products and services  
- F1.2: calculate compound interest and solve related problems  
- F1.3: analyze advantages/disadvantages of various savings, investment, credit, and payment methods  
- F1.4: explain how financial planning decisions are influenced by personal and societal factors:contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

## Cross-Strand Task (optional)
**Example**: *“Plan a Start-Up Business”* – students create a budget and investment plan (F1), calculate costs, revenues, and percent profit (B2, B1.3), model linear sales growth (C1), design graphs (D1), and scale packaging dimensions (E2).
