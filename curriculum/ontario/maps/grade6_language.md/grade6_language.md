# Grade 6 Language Curriculum Map

---

## Strand A: Literacy Connections and Applications
### Overall Expectation
- Apply transferable skills, digital media literacy, cross-curricular connections, and cultural awareness in literacy contexts.

**A1: Transferable Skills**
- A1.1: explain how transferable skills (critical thinking, innovation, self-directed learning, collaboration, communication, global citizenship, digital literacy) can be used to support communication in cultural, social, linguistic, and subject-specific contexts   
- A1.2: explain how transferable skills help them express voice, be engaged, and plan next steps for growth  

**A2: Digital Media Literacy**
- A2.1: explain rights/responsibilities when interacting online and developing digital identity  
- A2.2: manage online safety, privacy, and well-being responsibly  
- A2.3: gather, evaluate, and use information considering credibility, accuracy, and perspectives  
- A2.4: analyze forms/techniques in digital/media texts and apply to their own creations  
- A2.5: explain relationships between form, message, audience, and purpose  
- A2.6: use digital tools for authentic problem-solving and innovative design  
- A2.7: collaborate respectfully and inclusively with online communities 

**A3: Applications, Connections, and Contributions**
- A3.1: apply language skills across subject areas and real-world contexts  
- A3.2: explore diverse voices and perspectives, including Canadian contexts  
- A3.3: demonstrate understanding of First Nations, Métis, and Inuit voices and legacies 

---

## Strand B: Foundations of Language
### Overall Expectation
- Strengthen oral communication, reading, writing, and conventions for effective communication.

**B1: Oral and Non-Verbal Communication**
- B1.1: listen actively, interpret ideas, and respond appropriately  
- B1.2: use comprehension strategies before, during, and after listening  
- B1.3: identify purpose and adapt messages to audience  
- B1.4: apply oral and non-verbal strategies (tone, pacing, gestures) for impact  
- B1.5: use precise word choice, varied sentence structures, and both active/passive voice to support communication 

**B2: Language Foundations for Reading and Writing**
- B2.1: use morphological knowledge (bases, prefixes, suffixes) to decode and spell complex words accurately  
- B2.2: expand vocabulary across contexts using word origins and morphology  
- B2.3: read a variety of texts fluently, with appropriate pacing, accuracy, and prosody  

**B3: Language Conventions**
- B3.1: construct complex sentences with adjective/relative clauses  
- B3.2: understand functions of parts of speech (gerunds, active/passive voice) and apply in texts  
- B3.3: use punctuation/capitalization to clarify meaning, including colons, commas after transitions, and proper dialogue formatting 

---

## Strand C: Comprehension – Understanding and Responding to Texts
### Overall Expectation
- Apply comprehension strategies to analyze meaning in a wide range of texts.

**C1: Knowledge about Texts**
- C1.1: interpret diverse text forms and genres, including digital/media texts by creators with diverse perspectives  
- C1.2: analyze patterns, features, and stylistic elements (tone, figurative language, bias)  
- C1.3: examine how images, text, and design features work together to create meaning 

**C2: Comprehension Strategies**
- C2.1: activate prior knowledge, predict, and question during reading  
- C2.2: summarize ideas and supporting details, paraphrase, and visualize  
- C2.3: monitor comprehension and apply fix-up strategies  

**C3: Critical Thinking in Literacy**
- C3.1: evaluate texts, forming evidence-based opinions  
- C3.2: make meaningful text-to-self, text-to-text, and text-to-world connections  
- C3.3: analyze representation and omission of perspectives in texts  
- C3.4: reflect on how diverse voices impact understanding 

---

## Strand D: Composition – Expressing Ideas and Creating Texts
### Overall Expectation
- Plan, draft, revise, edit, and present a range of original texts.

**D1: Developing Ideas**
- D1.1: generate ideas using personal experience, inquiry, and research  
- D1.2: use organizers and strategies to plan text structures  
- D1.3: gather and evaluate information, sequencing content appropriately  
- D1.4: reflect on strategies for organizing ideas  

**D2: Creating Texts**
- D2.1: draft narrative, persuasive, and informational texts using print, digital, and media forms  
- D2.2: demonstrate fluency in cursive, keyboarding, and word processing  
- D2.3: establish voice and adjust tone/style for purpose and audience  
- D2.4: identify bias and point of view in their own writing  
- D2.5: revise for clarity and coherence  
- D2.6: edit for grammar, punctuation, and spelling  

**D3: Publishing, Presenting, and Reflecting**
- D3.1: produce polished texts using appropriate media and techniques  
- D3.2: present effectively using print, oral, and digital tools, explaining communication choices  
- D3.3: reflect on strategies/tools that supported publishing/presenting 

---

## Cross-Strand Task (optional)
**Example**: *“Global Issues Blog Project”* – students research global issues (A3.2, A2.3), read informational/digital texts (C1), draft blog posts with voice and style (D2), apply grammar and conventions (B3), and publish online to a wider audience (D3).
