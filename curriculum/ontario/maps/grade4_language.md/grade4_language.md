# Grade 4 Language Curriculum Map

---

## Strand A: Literacy Connections and Applications
### Overall Expectation
- Apply transferable skills, develop digital media literacy, connect learning across contexts, and engage with diverse perspectives.

**A1: Transferable Skills**
- A1.1: explain how transferable skills support communication in cultural, social, linguistic, and subject-specific contexts, and apply them in reading, writing, speaking, and creating  
- A1.2: explain how transferable skills help express voice, foster engagement, and plan next steps for growth 

**A2: Digital Media Literacy**
- A2.1: explain rights and responsibilities when engaging online  
- A2.2: use safe, responsible practices when navigating digital environments  
- A2.3: gather, evaluate, and synthesize information from diverse sources  
- A2.4: analyze conventions and techniques in digital and media texts  
- A2.5: explain relationships among form, message, context, audience, and creator  
- A2.6: use digital tools in design processes to create innovative solutions  

**A3: Applications, Connections, and Contributions**
- A3.1: explain how language knowledge supports learning across subjects and in everyday life  
- A3.2: explore contributions and perspectives of diverse individuals and communities, including Canadian contexts  
- A3.3: explain themes from First Nations, Métis, and Inuit cultures to show understanding of identities, perspectives, legacies, and ways of knowing 

---

## Strand B: Foundations of Language
### Overall Expectation
- Apply listening, speaking, reading, writing, and language conventions to communicate and build knowledge.

**B1: Oral and Non-Verbal Communication**
- B1.1: use effective listening skills (asking questions, responding in discussions)  
- B1.2: apply strategies before, during, after listening to comprehend information  
- B1.3: identify audience and purpose for speaking, using strategies for clarity (paraphrasing, restating)  
- B1.4: use oral and non-verbal strategies (expression, gestures, body language) to enhance meaning  
- B1.5: use varied word choice, adjectives/adverbs, sentence types, and cohesion to support comprehension 

**B2: Language Foundations for Reading and Writing**
- B2.1: use morphological knowledge (prefixes, suffixes, roots) to read and spell  
- B2.2: apply vocabulary strategies to expand word knowledge and usage  
- B2.3: read fluently with accuracy, expression, and pacing  

**B3: Language Conventions**
- B3.1: construct compound and complex sentences with clarity  
- B3.2: demonstrate knowledge of functions of parts of speech in sentences  
- B3.3: apply correct punctuation (commas in lists, quotation marks, apostrophes) and capitalization 

---

## Strand C: Comprehension – Understanding and Responding to Texts
### Overall Expectation
- Use comprehension strategies to interpret meaning from a variety of texts.

**C1: Knowledge about Texts**
- C1.1: read and comprehend a variety of texts, including digital/media texts by diverse creators  
- C1.2: analyze text forms and genres (stories, reports, cultural texts) and their features  
- C1.3: analyze text patterns (cause/effect, sequence) and features (captions, diagrams)  
- C1.4: evaluate how visuals and design contribute to meaning  
- C1.5: identify elements of style (voice, sentence structure, vocabulary) 

**C2: Comprehension Strategies**
- C2.1: activate prior knowledge and make predictions  
- C2.2: ask and answer questions to monitor understanding  
- C2.3: summarize ideas and supporting details  
- C2.4: apply strategies before, during, and after reading to deepen comprehension  

**C3: Critical Thinking in Literacy**
- C3.1: form opinions about texts with supporting reasons  
- C3.2: make text-to-self, text-to-text, and text-to-world connections  
- C3.3: analyze whose perspectives are included or missing in texts  
- C3.4: engage respectfully with diverse voices and ideas  

---

## Strand D: Composition – Expressing Ideas and Creating Texts
### Overall Expectation
- Create texts by planning, drafting, revising, editing, and presenting for a variety of audiences and purposes.

**D1: Developing Ideas**
- D1.1: generate and sort ideas from personal experience, research, and imagination  
- D1.2: use graphic organizers and planning tools to organize ideas  

**D2: Creating Texts**
- D2.1: draft texts in different forms (narrative, persuasive, procedural, report, digital)  
- D2.2: begin to use cursive writing with consistent legibility   
- D2.3: demonstrate personal voice using descriptive vocabulary and sentence variety  
- D2.4: identify and apply point of view (first or third person)  
- D2.5: revise drafts by adding detail and improving clarity  
- D2.6: edit and proofread for grammar, spelling, and punctuation  

**D3: Publishing, Presenting, and Reflecting**
- D3.1: create final texts using effective techniques and tools  
- D3.2: present texts orally with appropriate pacing, tone, and expression  
- D3.3: reflect on strategies used and explain how they helped improve as text creators 

---

## Cross-Strand Task (optional)
**Example**: *“Heritage Stories Project”* – students research a cultural or community story (A3.2, A3.3), read related texts (C1), draft narratives or reports (D2), apply vocabulary and conventions (B2, B3), and present their work through oral storytelling or digital media (D3).
