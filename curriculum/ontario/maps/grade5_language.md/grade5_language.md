# Grade 5 Language Curriculum Map

---

## Strand A: Literacy Connections and Applications
### Overall Expectation
- Apply transferable skills, digital media literacy, cross-curricular connections, and cultural awareness in language contexts.

**A1: Transferable Skills**
- A1.1: explain how transferable skills support communication in cultural, social, linguistic, and subject-specific contexts; apply them in reading, listening, viewing, and creating texts   
- A1.2: explain how transferable skills help express voice, foster engagement, and plan next steps for growth   

**A2: Digital Media Literacy**
- A2.1: explain rights/responsibilities when interacting online  
- A2.2: navigate digital environments safely, protect privacy, and support well-being  
- A2.3: gather, evaluate, and use information, considering credibility and perspectives   
- A2.4: analyze conventions and techniques in digital/media texts and apply to their own creations  
- A2.5: explain relationships among form, message, context, audience, and purpose   
- A2.6: use digital tools to support design and create solutions  
- A2.7: communicate and collaborate respectfully in online communities   

**A3: Applications, Connections, and Contributions**
- A3.1: apply language skills across subjects and real-world contexts  
- A3.2: explore contributions and perspectives of diverse communities, including Canadian contexts  
- A3.3: identify and explain themes from First Nations, Métis, and Inuit cultures to show understanding of perspectives and legacies   

---

## Strand B: Foundations of Language
### Overall Expectation
- Strengthen listening, speaking, reading, writing, and conventions for communication.

**B1: Oral and Non-Verbal Communication**
- B1.1: listen actively and respond appropriately in discussions  
- B1.2: apply strategies before, during, and after listening to support comprehension  
- B1.3: identify purpose and audience for speaking; adapt message accordingly  
- B1.4: use oral/non-verbal strategies (tone, gestures, volume, emphasis) to enhance communication  
- B1.5: use varied vocabulary and sentence structures to express ideas clearly  

**B2: Language Foundations for Reading and Writing**
- B2.1: apply phonics, morphology, and etymology to decode/spell unfamiliar words  
- B2.2: expand vocabulary by using context, word origins, and subject-specific terms  
- B2.3: read fluently with accuracy, phrasing, and expression  

**B3: Language Conventions**
- B3.1: construct clear compound and complex sentences  
- B3.2: apply knowledge of grammar and parts of speech (pronouns, conjunctions, adverbs)  
- B3.3: use accurate punctuation (commas, quotation marks, apostrophes) and capitalization  

---

## Strand C: Comprehension – Understanding and Responding to Texts
### Overall Expectation
- Apply comprehension strategies to interpret meaning from diverse texts.

**C1: Knowledge about Texts**
- C1.1: read and comprehend a range of texts (narrative, persuasive, informational, digital/media)  
- C1.2: analyze forms and genres, describing their features  
- C1.3: analyze text patterns (cause/effect, problem/solution, compare/contrast) and structures  
- C1.4: evaluate how images and text elements work together  
- C1.5: identify and analyze elements of style (voice, tone, figurative language)  

**C2: Comprehension Strategies**
- C2.1: activate prior knowledge, make predictions, and question while reading  
- C2.2: summarize key ideas and supporting details  
- C2.3: apply strategies such as visualization, paraphrasing, and note-taking  

**C3: Critical Thinking in Literacy**
- C3.1: evaluate texts by stating opinions and justifying them with evidence  
- C3.2: make text-to-self, text-to-text, and text-to-world connections  
- C3.3: analyze perspectives included and excluded in texts  
- C3.4: reflect on the impact of diverse voices on their own understanding  

---

## Strand D: Composition – Expressing Ideas and Creating Texts
### Overall Expectation
- Plan, draft, revise, edit, and present original texts in multiple forms and media.

**D1: Developing Ideas**
- D1.1: generate ideas using brainstorming, research, and personal experiences  
- D1.2: use organizers and strategies to plan text structures  
- D1.3: select and classify information, sequencing content according to form/genre   
- D1.4: reflect on strategies used for organizing ideas  

**D2: Creating Texts**
- D2.1: draft narrative, persuasive, and informational texts using various media and tools   
- D2.2: write fluent cursive, keyboard with automaticity, and apply word-processing skills   
- D2.3: establish personal voice, using varied language and tone appropriate to genre   
- D2.4: identify perspectives and bias in their own texts; explain how audiences may interpret them   
- D2.5: revise content for clarity, focus, and coherence   
- D2.6: edit for grammar, punctuation, spelling, and style   

**D3: Publishing, Presenting, and Reflecting**
- D3.1: produce polished texts using print, oral, and digital tools   
- D3.2: present texts effectively using media and explain how choices supported communication   
- D3.3: reflect on strategies/tools that supported publishing/presenting and plan next steps   

---

## Cross-Strand Task (optional)
**Example**: *“Community Voices Magazine”* – students research community issues (A3, A2.3), read informational texts (C1), draft articles with personal voice (D2), apply grammar/conventions (B3), and publish a class magazine online (D3).
