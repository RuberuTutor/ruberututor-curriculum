# Grade 2 Language Curriculum Map

---

## Strand A: Literacy Connections and Applications
### Overall Expectation
- Apply the seven transferable skills in language and literacy contexts, develop digital media literacy skills, apply language learning in other contexts, and engage with diverse perspectives.

**A1: Transferable Skills**
- A1.1: identify how transferable skills can be used to support communication in cultural, social, linguistic, and subject-specific contexts  
- A1.2: demonstrate how transferable skills help express voice and engagement in learning  

**A2: Digital Media Literacy**
- A2.1: demonstrate an understanding of rights and responsibilities when interacting online  
- A2.2: navigate online environments safely, manage privacy, and support well-being  
- A2.3: gather, evaluate, and use information from digital sources  
- A2.4: recognize forms, conventions, and techniques of digital/media texts  
- A2.5: explain interrelationships among form, message, context, and audience  
- A2.6: use digital tools in design processes to solve problems  

**A3: Applications, Connections, and Contributions**
- A3.1: apply language skills in other subjects and everyday life  
- A3.2: demonstrate understanding of diverse contributions and perspectives, including Canadian contexts  
- A3.3: identify themes in First Nations, Métis, and Inuit cultures to understand perspectives and ways of knowing:contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

## Strand B: Foundations of Language
### Overall Expectation
- Apply listening, speaking, reading, writing, and conventions to build language knowledge.

**B1: Oral and Non-Verbal Communication**
- B1.1: use effective listening skills (questions, restating, showing interest)  
- B1.2: use strategies before, during, after listening to understand oral messages  
- B1.3: identify purpose and audience for speaking, and use strategies to stay on topic  
- B1.4: apply oral and non-verbal strategies (gestures, body language) to enhance meaning  
- B1.5: use vocabulary, grammar, and cohesive sentences when speaking:contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

**B2: Language Foundations for Reading and Writing**
- B2.1: use phonics, syllabication, and morphology to decode and spell familiar words  
- B2.2: acquire and use vocabulary in oral and written contexts  
- B2.3: read simple texts fluently, with appropriate pace and expression  

**B3: Language Conventions**
- B3.1: construct simple and compound sentences to express ideas  
- B3.2: demonstrate knowledge of parts of speech in oral/written communication  
- B3.3: use capitals and punctuation appropriately (periods, question marks, exclamation marks)  

---

## Strand C: Comprehension – Understanding and Responding to Texts
### Overall Expectation
- Apply comprehension strategies to make meaning from various texts.

**C1: Knowledge about Texts**
- C1.1: read and understand different text forms and genres (stories, poems, information texts)  
- C1.2: describe features of text forms (headings, diagrams, labels)  
- C1.3: explain how visuals and text elements combine to create meaning  
- C1.4: identify elements of style (word choice, repetition)  

**C2: Comprehension Strategies**
- C2.1: use prior knowledge before reading  
- C2.2: ask and answer questions during reading  
- C2.3: summarize main ideas after reading  

**C3: Critical Thinking in Literacy**
- C3.1: express opinions about texts and justify them  
- C3.2: make personal connections to texts  
- C3.3: identify voices and perspectives represented or missing in texts  

---

## Strand D: Composition – Expressing Ideas and Creating Texts
### Overall Expectation
- Apply knowledge of text forms, drafting, revising, and presenting to create original texts.

**D1: Developing Ideas**
- D1.1: generate ideas for writing from personal experiences, imagination, and inquiry  
- D1.2: plan texts using pictures, organizers, and oral rehearsal  

**D2: Creating Texts**
- D2.1: draft texts of different forms (narratives, letters, simple persuasive/procedural)  
- D2.2: write legibly with spacing and consistent letter formation  
- D2.3: show personal voice and perspective in writing  
- D2.4: identify and use point of view (first person, third person)  
- D2.5: revise, edit, and proofread drafts (spelling, grammar, punctuation)  

**D3: Publishing, Presenting, and Reflecting**
- D3.1: produce final texts using appropriate media and techniques  
- D3.2: present texts aloud with expression  
- D3.3: reflect on strategies that helped during text creation or presentation:contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

## Cross-Strand Task (optional)
**Example**: *“Community Heroes Project”* – students research a community helper (A3.2), read stories or informational texts (C1), take notes using listening skills (B1.2), draft and revise a simple biography (D2), and present it using visuals and oral delivery (D3).
