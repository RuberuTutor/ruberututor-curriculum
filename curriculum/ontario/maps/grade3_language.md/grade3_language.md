# Grade 3 Language Curriculum Map

---

## Strand A: Literacy Connections and Applications
### Overall Expectation
- Apply the seven transferable skills, develop digital media literacy, connect language learning across contexts, and engage with diverse perspectives.

**A1: Transferable Skills**
- A1.1: identify how transferable skills support communication in cultural, social, linguistic, and subject-specific contexts  
- A1.2: explain how transferable skills help express voice and engagement in learning  

**A2: Digital Media Literacy**
- A2.1: demonstrate rights and responsibilities when interacting online, with appropriate permission  
- A2.2: navigate online environments safely, manage privacy, and support well-being  
- A2.3: gather, evaluate, and use information considering multiple perspectives  
- A2.4: explain forms, conventions, and techniques of digital/media texts and apply understanding when analyzing texts  
- A2.5: analyze relationships among form, message, context, audience, and creator  
- A2.6: use digital tools in the design process to develop creative solutions 

**A3: Applications, Connections, and Contributions**
- A3.1: apply language learning to other subjects and real-life contexts  
- A3.2: recognize diverse cultural contributions, including Canadian contexts  
- A3.3: identify themes and perspectives in First Nations, Métis, and Inuit cultures and explain how they shape understanding 

---

## Strand B: Foundations of Language
### Overall Expectation
- Build foundational listening, speaking, reading, writing, and conventions knowledge.

**B1: Oral and Non-Verbal Communication**
- B1.1: listen actively and respond appropriately  
- B1.2: use comprehension strategies before, during, and after listening  
- B1.3: identify purpose and audience for speaking, and adapt accordingly  
- B1.4: use oral and non-verbal strategies (e.g., emphasis, gestures, tone) to enhance meaning  
- B1.5: use vocabulary and cohesive sentences to communicate ideas  

**B2: Language Foundations for Reading and Writing**
- B2.1: use phonics, morphology, and syllabication to decode/spell words  
- B2.2: expand vocabulary and apply in oral/written contexts  
- B2.3: read with fluency and appropriate intonation  

**B3: Language Conventions**
- B3.1: use compound and complex sentences to express ideas  
- B3.2: apply knowledge of parts of speech (nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, pronouns, conjunctions)  
- B3.3: use punctuation (periods, question marks, commas in lists) and capitalization accurately  

---

## Strand C: Comprehension – Understanding and Responding to Texts
### Overall Expectation
- Apply comprehension strategies to interpret a variety of texts.

**C1: Knowledge about Texts**
- C1.1: read and comprehend a range of text forms (stories, informational, poetry)  
- C1.2: describe text features (captions, tables of contents, glossaries)  
- C1.3: analyze visuals and text elements together  
- C1.4: recognize and describe elements of style (word choice, sentence rhythm)  

**C2: Comprehension Strategies**
- C2.1: make predictions, ask/answer questions, clarify meaning during reading  
- C2.2: summarize ideas and key details  
- C2.3: use graphic organizers and strategies to support understanding  

**C3: Critical Thinking in Literacy**
- C3.1: evaluate texts by expressing opinions with supporting reasons  
- C3.2: make text-to-self, text-to-text, and text-to-world connections  
- C3.3: analyze voices and perspectives included/excluded in texts 

---

## Strand D: Composition – Expressing Ideas and Creating Texts
### Overall Expectation
- Plan, draft, revise, and present original texts in different forms.

**D1: Developing Ideas**
- D1.1: generate ideas from personal experience, imagination, and research  
- D1.2: organize ideas using graphic organizers or outlines  

**D2: Creating Texts**
- D2.1: draft texts in different forms (narrative, persuasive, report, procedural)  
- D2.2: print and begin to write in cursive with legibility  
- D2.3: show personal voice in writing, using descriptive vocabulary  
- D2.4: identify and apply point of view in texts (first or third person)  
- D2.5: revise texts by adding details and improving clarity  
- D2.6: edit and proofread for grammar, punctuation, and spelling 

**D3: Publishing, Presenting, and Reflecting**
- D3.1: create polished final texts using effective techniques and tools  
- D3.2: present texts orally, using volume, pacing, and expression  
- D3.3: reflect on strategies that helped during text creation or presentation 

---

## Cross-Strand Task (optional)
**Example**: *“My Community Newspaper”* – students research a local issue (A2.3, A3.1), read informational texts (C1), draft articles with revisions (D2), apply conventions (B3), and present a class newspaper (D3).
