# MBF3C — Foundations for College Mathematics, Grade 11 (College Preparation)

## 1) Strands Overview
- A. Number Sense and Algebra
- B. Measurement and Geometry
- C. Trigonometry
- D. Data Management
- E. Financial Mathematics

## 2) Overall Expectations

### Strand A — Number Sense and Algebra
By the end of this course, students will:
- demonstrate an understanding of the characteristics of quadratic functions, and make connections between the numeric, graphical, and algebraic representations of quadratic relations;
- simplify polynomial, rational, and radical expressions, and solve polynomial and simple rational equations;
- demonstrate an understanding of the characteristics of exponential functions, and make connections between the numeric, graphical, and algebraic representations of exponential relations.

### Strand B — Measurement and Geometry
By the end of this course, students will:
- solve problems involving measurement and geometry and problems involving the trigonometry of right and acute triangles.

### Strand C — Trigonometry
By the end of this course, students will:
- solve problems involving trigonometry in acute triangles, using the sine law and the cosine law, including problems arising from real-world applications.

### Strand D — Data Management
By the end of this course, students will:
- organize, interpret, and analyze data using numerical summaries, and present the results in a variety of forms;
- make inferences and convincing arguments based on the analysis of data;
- demonstrate an understanding of the concepts of probability and the applications of probability distributions.

### Strand E — Financial Mathematics
By the end of this course, students will:
- solve problems involving compound interest and ordinary annuities, including problems involving compound interest and annuities connected with real-world applications.

## 3) Specific Expectations

### Strand A — Number Sense and Algebra
- A1.1 expand and simplify polynomial expressions involving monomials, binomials, and trinomials, and collect like terms;
- A1.2 factor polynomial expressions involving common factors, trinomials, and differences of squares;
- A1.3 simplify rational expressions by identifying non-permissible values and by reducing to lowest terms;
- A1.4 simplify radical expressions by using exponent laws for integer exponents, and evaluate radicals;
- A1.5 solve quadratic equations by factoring, by using the quadratic formula, and by graphing;
- A1.6 solve simple rational equations, and describe restrictions on solutions;
- A1.7 solve problems involving quadratic equations, and interpret solutions in context;
- A1.8 recognize exponential equations in real-world applications, and solve simple exponential equations by graphing or using technology.

### Strand B — Measurement and Geometry
- B1.1 solve problems involving the surface area and volume of three-dimensional figures, including spheres, cones, cylinders, and pyramids;
- B1.2 solve problems involving scale diagrams and similar triangles;
- B1.3 solve problems involving the Pythagorean theorem in two and three dimensions.

### Strand C — Trigonometry
- C1.1 solve problems involving acute triangles by applying the sine law and the cosine law;
- C1.2 solve problems that require the use of trigonometric ratios in two and three dimensions.

### Strand D — Data Management
- D1.1 collect, organize, and display data, using a variety of methods and appropriate technology;
- D1.2 determine, using appropriate technology, measures of central tendency and measures of spread;
- D1.3 interpret, draw conclusions from, and make decisions based on data presented in a variety of forms;
- D1.4 recognize and explain the use of sampling techniques in the collection of data;
- D1.5 demonstrate an understanding of the concepts of probability and use them to solve problems involving discrete sample spaces.

### Strand E — Financial Mathematics
- E1.1 solve problems involving compound interest, using appropriate technology;
- E1.2 solve problems involving ordinary annuities, using appropriate technology;
- E1.3 solve problems involving ordinary annuities connected with real-world applications.
