## Strand D. Composition: Expressing Ideas and Creating Texts

In this strand, students use the writing and creating processes to plan, research, draft, revise, edit, proofread, publish, and share texts of various forms and genres for a variety of purposes and audiences. They apply knowledge of language conventions in their writing, and use feedback to refine and improve their texts. Students select and use appropriate media, tools, and techniques to create and present texts and to reflect on their effectiveness.

**Overall expectations**  
Throughout this course, in connection with the learning in strands A to C, students will:  

**D1. Developing Ideas and Organizing Content**  
plan, develop ideas, gather information, and organize content for creating texts of various forms, including digital and media texts, on a variety of topics  

**D2. Creating Texts**  
apply knowledge and understanding of various text forms and genres to create, revise, edit, and proofread their own texts, using a variety of media, tools, and strategies, and reflect critically on created texts  

**D3. Publishing, Presenting, and Reflecting**  
select suitable and effective media, techniques, and tools to publish and present final texts, and critically analyze how well the texts address various topics  

---

**Specific expectations**

**D1.1** generate, develop, and organize ideas to write and create texts that are appropriate for the purpose and audience  

**D1.2** locate and select relevant information and ideas from various sources to support the development of their texts  

**D1.3** use the writing and creating processes, including planning, drafting, revising, editing, and proofreading, to create texts of various forms and genres  

**D1.4** use feedback from various sources to revise and improve their texts  

**D1.5** reflect on their strengths, areas for growth, and goals as creators of texts  

**D2.1** select and use appropriate text forms, genres, and media to create texts that communicate clearly and effectively for different audiences and purposes  

**D2.2** use voice, point of view, and elements of style appropriately to create texts that are engaging, coherent, and effective  

**D2.3** revise texts to improve content, organization, clarity, and accuracy, and to ensure that texts are suitable for the intended audience and purpose  

**D2.4** edit and proofread to correct errors in grammar, usage, and conventions and to improve overall effectiveness  

**D2.5** use language conventions accurately and effectively to communicate meaning clearly  

**D2.6** use appropriate media, tools, and strategies to create texts and to demonstrate learning  

**D3.1** select appropriate media, techniques, and tools to publish and present final texts for specific audiences and purposes  

**D3.2** present and share texts clearly and effectively, using appropriate techniques to engage audiences  

**D3.3** evaluate and reflect on the effectiveness of their texts in meeting their purposes and addressing various topics  
## Strand C. Comprehension: Understanding and Responding to Texts

In this strand, students apply foundational knowledge and skills and demonstrate an understanding of how texts are constructed and how they communicate meaning. They read, listen to, and view a variety of texts by creators with diverse identities, perspectives, and experiences. Students analyze, interpret, and evaluate texts to deepen their understanding and to make connections to themselves, to other texts, and to the world. They apply comprehension strategies before, during, and after reading, listening, and viewing to clarify meaning and to monitor their understanding. Students also think critically about texts to analyze how perspectives and topics are communicated and addressed.

**Overall expectations**  
Throughout this course, in connection with the learning in strands A, B, and D, students will:  

**C1. Knowledge about Texts**  
apply foundational knowledge and skills to understand a variety of texts, including digital and media texts, by creators with diverse identities, perspectives, and experiences, and demonstrate an understanding of the patterns, features, and elements of style associated with various text forms and genres  

**C2. Comprehension Strategies**  
apply comprehension strategies before, during, and after engaging with a variety of texts to understand and clarify meaning  

**C3. Critical Thinking in Literacy**  
apply critical thinking skills to deepen understanding of texts and analyze how various perspectives and topics are communicated and addressed in a variety of texts, including digital, media, and cultural texts  

---

**Specific expectations**

**C1.1** read and comprehend various complex texts, using knowledge of words, grammar, cohesive ties, sentence structures, and background knowledge  

**C1.2** analyze and compare the characteristics of various text forms and genres, including cultural text forms, and provide evidence to explain how they help communicate meaning  

**C1.3** compare the text patterns and text features associated with different text forms, including cultural texts, and evaluate their importance in helping readers, listeners, and viewers understand the meaning  

**C1.4** evaluate how images, graphics, and visual design create, communicate, and contribute to meaning in a variety of texts  

**C1.5** identify various elements of style in texts, including voice, word choice, word patterns, and sentence structure, and analyze how each element helps create meaning and is appropriate for the text form and genre  

**C1.6** analyze the narrator’s point of view and the perspectives of creators and characters in texts and suggest alternative points of view, giving reasons  

**C1.7** read, listen to, and view a wide variety of text forms by diverse First Nations, Métis, and Inuit creators to make meaning through Indigenous storywork and to respect protocols for engaging with First Nations, Métis, and Inuit texts and knowledge  

**C2.1** identify and explain prior knowledge and personal experiences and make connections to new information to make meaning and understand new texts  

**C2.2** identify a variety of purposes for engaging with texts and select texts to meet their purposes and to support their learning, and explain why the selections are appropriate  

**C2.3** make predictions about texts and pose questions to confirm or refute their predictions, and revise or refine their understanding as indicated  

**C2.4** monitor and reflect on their understanding of texts and identify and apply appropriate strategies to clarify meaning as they read, listen to, and view  

**C2.5** use a variety of before-, during-, and after-reading strategies, including summarizing, note making, and synthesizing, to understand and clarify the meaning of texts  

**C2.6** explain how they use metacognitive strategies to monitor their comprehension and adjust their strategies as needed to deepen understanding  

**C2.7** synthesize information and ideas from multiple texts to deepen understanding and to develop and communicate new knowledge  

**C3.1** identify and analyze how perspectives, including their own, are represented in texts and how they influence meaning  

**C3.2** identify bias and analyze how it is used in texts, including digital and media texts  

**C3.3** evaluate the credibility, reliability, currency, and quality of information and ideas in texts, including digital and media texts  

**C3.4** analyze the ways creators use language, images, and other conventions to communicate messages, influence audiences, and achieve specific purposes  

**C3.5** draw conclusions and support them with evidence from texts, and reflect on how their own perspectives influence their interpretations  
## Strand B. Foundations of Language

In this strand, students consolidate foundational knowledge and skills that are essential for reading and writing. They apply these skills in a variety of contexts across the curriculum and beyond. Students use word-level reading and spelling knowledge and vocabulary to read, understand, and create texts with increasing fluency, accuracy, and automaticity. They also consolidate their understanding of language conventions and apply this knowledge as they read and create sentences and paragraphs and a variety of texts for different purposes and audiences. They learn that effective communication requires the integration of a range of strategies and skills, including word choice and syntax, and that language conventions are the building blocks of communication. Oral and non-verbal communication, reading, and writing are interconnected, and students develop and use oral and non-verbal communication skills to support comprehension and composition.

**Overall expectations**  
Throughout this course, in connection with the learning in strands A, C, and D, students will:  

**B1. Oral and Non-Verbal Communication**  
apply listening, speaking, and non-verbal communication skills and strategies to understand and communicate meaning in formal and informal contexts and for various purposes and audiences  

**B2. Language Foundations for Reading and Writing**  
demonstrate an understanding of foundational language knowledge and skills, and apply this understanding when reading and writing  

**B3. Language Conventions for Reading and Writing**  
demonstrate an understanding of sentence structure, grammar, cohesive ties, and capitalization and punctuation, and apply this knowledge when reading and writing sentences, paragraphs, and a variety of texts  

---

**Specific expectations**

**B1.1** listen actively to understand and respond appropriately to various forms of communication in a variety of contexts, for a variety of purposes, and from a variety of perspectives  

**B1.2** use appropriate speaking skills and strategies to communicate effectively in a variety of contexts, for a variety of purposes, and to a variety of audiences  

**B1.3** demonstrate an understanding of how non-verbal communication (e.g., tone, volume, intonation, pitch, pace, gestures, facial expressions, body language, use of space) contributes to meaning  

**B1.4** use non-verbal communication strategies to support and enhance verbal communication in a variety of contexts and for a variety of purposes  

**B1.5** reflect on and evaluate their oral and non-verbal communication skills to improve effectiveness as communicators in a variety of contexts  

**B1.6** demonstrate an understanding of the connections between listening, speaking, reading, and writing skills, and apply this knowledge in a variety of contexts  

**B2.1** use consolidated knowledge of the meanings of words and morphemes (i.e., bases, prefixes, and suffixes) to read and spell complex words with accuracy and automaticity  

**B2.2** demonstrate an understanding of a wide variety of words, acquire and use explicitly taught vocabulary flexibly in various contexts, including other subject areas, and use consolidated morphological knowledge to analyze and understand new words in context  

**B2.3** read a variety of complex texts fluently, with accuracy and appropriate pacing to support comprehension, and when reading aloud, adjust expression and intonation according to the purpose of reading  

**B3.1** use their knowledge of sentence types and forms to construct complex sentences that connect and communicate ideas accurately and effectively  

**B3.2** demonstrate an understanding of the functions of parts of speech in sentences, consolidate and refine their understanding of grammar, and use this knowledge to support comprehension and communicate meaning clearly and precisely  

**B3.3** use and refine their understanding of the meaning and function of capitalization and punctuation to communicate meaning clearly and coherently when reading and writing  
## Strand A. Literacy Connections and Applications

In this strand, students strengthen their understanding of how language and literacy are connected to identity and contribute to community. They examine how their lived experiences and identities and those of others are rooted in families, communities, groups, and nations. They develop this knowledge by engaging with a diversity of texts and by reading, listening to, viewing, and creating texts for a variety of purposes and audiences. They use transferable skills when reading, listening to, viewing, and creating texts of various forms. They also use digital and media tools and strategies to research, construct knowledge, demonstrate their learning, and collaborate. Students learn to interact safely and responsibly in online environments. They develop the knowledge and skills needed to be critical consumers and creators of media and to communicate and collaborate with others in inclusive, respectful, and responsible ways.

**Overall expectations**  
Throughout this course, in connection with the learning in strands B to D, students will:  

**A1. Transferable Skills**  
demonstrate an understanding of how the seven transferable skills (critical thinking and problem solving; innovation, creativity, and entrepreneurship; self-directed learning; collaboration; communication; global citizenship and sustainability; and digital literacy) are used in various language and literacy contexts  

**A2. Digital Media Literacy**  
demonstrate and apply the knowledge and skills needed to interact safely and responsibly in online environments, use digital and media tools to construct knowledge, and demonstrate learning as critical consumers and creators of media  

**A3. Applications, Connections, and Contributions**  
apply language and literacy skills in cross-curricular and integrated learning, and demonstrate an understanding of, and make connections to, diverse voices, experiences, perspectives, histories, and contributions, including those of First Nations, Métis, and Inuit individuals, communities, groups, and nations  

---

**Specific expectations**  

**A1.1** analyze and explain how transferable skills can be used to support communication in various cultural, social, linguistic, and domain-specific contexts, and apply them when reading, listening to, viewing, and creating texts of various forms  

**A1.2** evaluate and explain how transferable skills help them to express their voice, be engaged in their learning, and implement a plan to develop their capabilities and potential  

**A2.1** evaluate and explain their rights and responsibilities when interacting online with appropriate permission, and make decisions that contribute positively to the development of their digital identity and those of their communities  

**A2.2** demonstrate an understanding of how to navigate online environments safely, manage their privacy, personal data, and security, and interact in a way that supports their well-being and that of others, including seeking appropriate permission  

**A2.3** conduct research, considering accuracy, credibility, and perspectives, with a focus on misinformation, disinformation, and curated information, to construct knowledge, create texts, and demonstrate learning, while respecting legal and ethical considerations  

**A2.4** evaluate the use of the various forms, conventions, and techniques of digital and media texts, consider the impact on the audience, and apply this understanding when analyzing and creating texts  

**A2.5** demonstrate an understanding of the interrelationships between the form, message, and context of texts, the intended and unintended audience, and the purpose for production  

**A2.6** select and use appropriate digital and media tools to support the design process and address authentic, relevant, real-world problems by developing and proposing innovative solutions  

**A2.7** communicate and collaborate with various communities in a safe, respectful, responsible, and inclusive manner when using online platforms and environments, including digital and media tools, and demonstrate and model cultural awareness with members of the community  

**A3.1** analyze and explain how the knowledge and skills developed in this course support learning in various subject areas and in everyday life, and describe how they enhance understanding and communication  

**A3.2** demonstrate an understanding of the historical contexts, contributions, lived experiences, and perspectives of a diversity of individuals and communities, including those in Canada, by exploring and analyzing the concepts of identity, self, and sense of belonging in a variety of culturally responsive and relevant texts  

**A3.3** analyze themes explored in First Nations, Métis, and Inuit cultures to demonstrate an understanding of the varied identities, perspectives, relationships, legacies, truths, and ways of knowing, being, and doing
