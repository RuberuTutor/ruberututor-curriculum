# ENG2D — English, Grade 10 (Academic)

## 1) Strands Overview
- A. Oral Communication
- B. Reading and Literature Studies
- C. Writing
- D. Media Studies

## 2) Overall Expectations

### Strand A — Oral Communication
By the end of this course, students will:
- listen to understand and respond appropriately in a variety of situations for a variety of purposes;
- use speaking skills and strategies appropriately to communicate with different audiences for a variety of purposes;
- reflect on and identify their strengths as listeners and speakers, areas for improvement, and the strategies they found most helpful in oral communication situations; :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

### Strand B — Reading and Literature Studies
By the end of this course, students will:
- read and demonstrate an understanding of a variety of literary, informational, and graphic texts, using a range of strategies to construct meaning;
- recognize a variety of text forms, text features, and stylistic elements and demonstrate understanding of how they help communicate meaning;
- use knowledge of words and cueing systems to read fluently;
- reflect on and identify their strengths as readers, areas for improvement, and the strategies they found most helpful before, during, and after reading. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

### Strand C — Writing
By the end of this course, students will:
- generate, gather, and organize ideas and information to write for an intended purpose and audience;
- draft and revise their writing, using a variety of forms and stylistic elements appropriate for the purpose and audience;
- use editing, proofreading, and publishing strategies and knowledge of conventions to present their work effectively;
- reflect on and identify their strengths as writers, areas for improvement, and the strategies they found most helpful at different stages in the writing process. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

### Strand D — Media Studies
By the end of this course, students will:
- demonstrate an understanding of a variety of media texts;
- identify media forms and explain how the conventions and techniques associated with them are used to create meaning;
- create a variety of media texts for different purposes and audiences using appropriate forms and techniques;
- describe strategies used in interpreting and creating media texts, explain which ones were helpful, and identify steps they can take to improve as media interpreters and producers. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
