# ENG4E — English, Grade 12 (Workplace Preparation)

## 1) Strands Overview
- A. Oral Communication  
- B. Reading and Literature Studies  
- C. Writing  
- D. Media Studies  

## 2) Overall Expectations

### Strand A — Oral Communication  
By the end of this course, students will:  
- listen to understand and respond appropriately in a variety of situations for a variety of purposes;  
- use speaking skills and strategies appropriately to communicate with different audiences for workplace-related purposes;  
- reflect on and identify their strengths as listeners and speakers, areas for improvement, and strategies to enhance oral communication.

### Strand B — Reading and Literature Studies  
By the end of this course, students will:  
- read and demonstrate understanding of a range of informational and literary texts from various cultures and contexts;  
- demonstrate understanding of various forms, including fiction, non-fiction, drama, poetry, and informational texts;  
- identify and explain stylistic elements and how they contribute to clear, practical communication.

### Strand C — Writing  
By the end of this course, students will:  
- use print and electronic sources to gather ideas and information for personal, school, and workplace writing;  
- select and use appropriate informational and literary forms for intended purposes and audiences;  
- organize, revise, edit, and proofread writing for clarity, accuracy, and appropriate voice and style.

### Strand D — Media Studies  
By the end of this course, students will:  
- demonstrate understanding of media texts, forms, audiences, and production techniques;  
- analyze how conventions and techniques convey meaning in media;  
- create media texts for practical purposes and audiences;  
- reflect on strengths and set goals for improvement as media interpreters and producers.

## 3) Specific Expectations

### Strand A — Oral Communication  
- A1.1 listen to understand and respond appropriately in a variety of situations;  
- A1.2 use speaking skills and strategies effectively in workplace-related contexts;  
- A1.3 reflect on listener/speaker strengths, areas for improvement, and helpful strategies.

### Strand B — Reading and Literature Studies  
- B1.1 read and interpret various workplace-relevant informational and literary texts;  
- B1.2 describe how form, genre, and style help convey meaning;  
- B1.3 relate text features and style choices to accurate communication in practical contexts.

### Strand C — Writing  
- C1.1 gather and organize information from various sources for writing tasks;  
- C1.2 select written forms suited to workplace and personal communication (e.g. memos, reports, agendas);  
- C1.3 structure writing coherently, revise for clarity, and edit for conventions of standard Canadian English.

### Strand D — Media Studies  
- D1.1 interpret media texts considering purpose, audience, and production elements;  
- D1.2 analyze how media forms and techniques convey messages;  
- D1.3 create media texts for practical communication purposes;  
- D1.4 assess their media skills and identify areas for growth.
