# MHF4U — Advanced Functions

## 1) Strands Overview
- A. Characteristics of Functions
- B. Properties of Functions
- C. Exponential and Logarithmic Functions
- D. Trigonometric Functions
- E. Polynomial and Rational Functions

## 2) Overall Expectations

### Strand A — Characteristics of Functions
By the end of this course, students will:
- demonstrate an understanding of functions, their representations, and their inverses, and make connections between the representations;
- determine the zeros and the maximum and minimum values of polynomial functions, and solve related problems;
- demonstrate an understanding of key features of the graphs of rational functions, and represent rational functions graphically;
- determine the values of trigonometric ratios for angles less than 360°, and solve related problems.

### Strand B — Properties of Functions
By the end of this course, students will:
- demonstrate an understanding of the equivalence of algebraic expressions involving radical and rational exponents;
- simplify expressions containing exponents and evaluate numerical expressions involving exponents and radicals;
- solve exponential equations algebraically, and solve problems involving exponential models;
- demonstrate an understanding of logarithms and their properties, and solve related problems.

### Strand C — Exponential and Logarithmic Functions
By the end of this course, students will:
- evaluate logarithmic expressions and apply the laws of logarithms to simplify expressions;
- graph exponential and logarithmic functions, and describe key features of the graphs;
- solve problems using exponential and logarithmic functions, including real-world applications.

### Strand D — Trigonometric Functions
By the end of this course, students will:
- demonstrate an understanding of the relationship between the unit circle and trigonometric functions;
- graph trigonometric functions and describe key features of the graphs;
- solve problems involving trigonometric equations and identities.

### Strand E — Polynomial and Rational Functions
By the end of this course, students will:
- determine the zeros and the maximum and minimum values of polynomial functions, and solve related problems;
- graph polynomial and rational functions, and describe key features of the graphs;
- solve problems involving polynomial and rational functions, including applications.

## 3) Specific Expectations

### Strand A — Characteristics of Functions
- **A1.1** demonstrate an understanding of function notation and the concepts of domain and range, and apply these concepts in problem-solving contexts;
- **A1.2** interpret the meaning of a function in terms of its graph and the situation it models;
- **A1.3** determine whether a relation is a function, and justify the conclusion;
- **A1.4** determine the values of a function for given values of the independent variable;
- **A1.5** determine the inverse of a function, and make connections between the function and its inverse.

### Strand B — Properties of Functions
- **B1.1** demonstrate an understanding of the equivalence between expressions involving radicals and expressions involving rational exponents;
- **B1.2** simplify expressions involving rational exponents and radicals, and evaluate numerical expressions;
- **B1.3** solve equations involving exponential functions algebraically, and verify solutions;
- **B1.4** demonstrate an understanding of the properties of logarithms, and apply these properties to simplify expressions.

### Strand C — Exponential and Logarithmic Functions
- **C1.1** evaluate logarithmic expressions using the laws of logarithms;
- **C1.2** graph exponential and logarithmic functions, and describe their domain, range, intercepts, and asymptotes;
- **C1.3** solve problems using exponential and logarithmic functions in real-world applications.

### Strand D — Trigonometric Functions
- **D1.1** determine the exact values of trigonometric ratios for special angles in standard position;
- **D1.2** graph trigonometric functions, and describe their amplitude, period, and phase shift;
- **D1.3** solve problems involving trigonometric equations in degree and radian measure.

### Strand E — Polynomial and Rational Functions
- **E1.1** determine the zeros and the maximum and minimum values of polynomial functions, and solve related problems;
- **E1.2** graph polynomial and rational functions, identifying key features such as intercepts and asymptotes;
- **E1.3** solve problems involving polynomial and rational functions, including real-world applications.
