# ENG4U — English, Grade 12 (University)

## 1) Strands Overview
- A. Oral Communication
- B. Reading and Literature Studies
- C. Writing
- D. Media Studies

## 2) Overall Expectations

### Strand A — Oral Communication  
By the end of this course, students will:  
- listen in order to understand and respond appropriately in a variety of situations for a variety of purposes;  
- use speaking skills and strategies appropriately to communicate with different audiences for a variety of purposes;  
- reflect on and identify their strengths as listeners and speakers, areas for improvement, and the strategies they found most helpful in oral communication situations.  

### Strand B — Reading and Literature Studies  
By the end of this course, students will:  
- read and demonstrate an understanding of a variety of complex literary, informational, and graphic texts, using a range of strategies to construct meaning;  
- recognize a variety of text forms, text features, and stylistic elements and demonstrate understanding of how they help communicate meaning;  
- use knowledge of words and cueing systems to read fluently;  
- reflect on and identify their strengths as readers, areas for improvement, and the strategies they found most helpful before, during, and after reading.  

### Strand C — Writing  
By the end of this course, students will:  
- generate, gather, and organize ideas and information to write for an intended purpose and audience;  
- draft and revise their writing, using a variety of forms and stylistic elements appropriate for the purpose and audience;  
- use editing, proofreading, and publishing strategies and knowledge of conventions to present their work effectively;  
- reflect on and identify their strengths as writers, areas for improvement, and the strategies they found most helpful at different stages in the writing process.  

### Strand D — Media Studies  
By the end of this course, students will:  
- demonstrate an understanding of a variety of media texts;  
- identify media forms and explain how the conventions and techniques associated with them are used to create meaning;  
- create a variety of media texts for different purposes and audiences using appropriate forms, conventions, and techniques;  
- reflect on and identify their strengths as media interpreters and producers, areas for improvement, and the strategies they found most helpful.  

## 3) Specific Expectations

### Strand A — Oral Communication  
- **A1.1** listen to understand and interpret ideas and information in oral texts, including increasingly complex texts, accurately and fluently;  
- **A1.2** use speaking skills and strategies appropriately to communicate effectively in discussions and presentations;  
- **A1.3** reflect on and evaluate their strengths as listeners and speakers, and set goals for improvement.  

### Strand B — Reading and Literature Studies  
- **B1.1** read and interpret a variety of literary, informational, and graphic texts, using a range of strategies;  
- **B1.2** analyze text forms, features, and stylistic elements to explain how they help communicate meaning;  
- **B1.3** use knowledge of words and cueing systems to read fluently and accurately;  
- **B1.4** reflect on and evaluate their strengths as readers and set goals for improvement.  

### Strand C — Writing  
- **C1.1** generate, gather, and organize ideas and information to write for specific purposes and audiences;  
- **C1.2** draft and revise their writing, using a variety of literary and informational forms and stylistic elements appropriate for the purpose and audience;  
- **C1.3** use knowledge of conventions and strategies for editing, proofreading, and publishing to present their work effectively;  
- **C1.4** reflect on and evaluate their strengths as writers and set goals for improvement.  

### Strand D — Media Studies  
- **D1.1** demonstrate an understanding of a variety of media texts;  
- **D1.2** analyze how conventions and techniques are used in different media forms to communicate meaning;  
- **D1.3** create a variety of media texts for different purposes and audiences, using appropriate conventions and techniques;  
- **D1.4** reflect on and evaluate their strengths as media interpreters and producers and set goals for improvement.
