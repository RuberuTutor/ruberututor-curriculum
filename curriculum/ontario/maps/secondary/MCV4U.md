# MCF3M — Functions and Applications

## 1) Strands Overview
- A. Quadratic Functions  
- B. Exponential Functions  
- C. Trigonometric Functions  

## 2) Overall Expectations

### Strand A — Quadratic Functions  
By the end of this course, students will:  
- Expand and simplify quadratic expressions, solve quadratic equations, and relate the roots of a quadratic equation to the corresponding graph;  
- Demonstrate an understanding of functions, and make connections between the numeric, graphical, and algebraic representations of quadratic functions;  
- Solve problems involving quadratic functions, including problems arising from real-world applications. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}  

### Strand B — Exponential Functions  
By the end of this course, students will:  
- Simplify and evaluate numerical expressions involving exponents, and make connections between the numeric, graphical, and algebraic representations of exponential functions;  
- Identify and represent exponential functions, and solve problems involving exponential functions, including problems arising from real-world applications;  
- Demonstrate an understanding of compound interest and annuities, and solve related problems. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}  

### Strand C — Trigonometric Functions  
By the end of this course, students will:  
- Solve problems involving trigonometry in acute triangles using the sine law and the cosine law, including problems arising from real-world applications;  
- Demonstrate an understanding of periodic relationships and the sine function, and make connections between the numeric, graphical, and algebraic representations of sine functions;  
- Identify and represent sine functions, and solve problems involving sine functions, including problems arising from real-world applications. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}  

## 3) Specific Expectations

### Strand A — Quadratic Functions

By the end of the course, students will:

- A1.1 Expand and simplify quadratic expressions, solve quadratic equations, and relate the roots of a quadratic equation to the corresponding graph.  
- A1.2 Demonstrate an understanding of functions, and make connections between the numeric, graphical, and algebraic representations of quadratic functions.  
- A1.3 Solve problems involving quadratic functions, including problems arising from real-world applications.  

### Strand B — Exponential Functions

By the end of the course, students will:

- B1.1 Simplify and evaluate numerical expressions involving exponents, and make connections between the numeric, graphical, and algebraic representations of exponential functions.  
- B1.2 Identify and represent exponential functions, and solve problems involving exponential functions, including problems arising from real-world applications.  
- B1.3 Demonstrate an understanding of compound interest and annuities, and solve related problems.  

### Strand C — Trigonometric Functions

By the end of the course, students will:

- C1.1 Solve problems involving trigonometry in acute triangles using the sine law and the cosine law, including problems arising from real-world applications.  
- C1.2 Demonstrate an understanding of periodic relationships and the sine function, and make connections between the numeric, graphical, and algebraic representations of sine functions.  
- C1.3 Identify and represent sine functions, and solve problems involving sine functions, including problems arising from real-world applications.
